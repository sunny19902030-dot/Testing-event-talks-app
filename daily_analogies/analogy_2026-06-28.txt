### 🧸 Kid-Friendly Analogy:

Imagine there is a huge, super popular cake baking competition in town, and all the bakers are trying to bake the biggest, fluffiest birthday cakes. But there is a big problem: the only store in town that sells baking flour has run out!

The biggest, richest bakeries (like Apple and Microsoft) bought up almost all the baking flour months ago. They have plenty of flour locked in their pantries to keep baking cakes. 

But the smaller, family-owned bakeries are in big trouble—they can't get any flour at all, and without it, they can't bake cakes or stay open! 

This is what is happening with "computer memory" chips right now. The biggest tech companies bought them all, leaving the smaller tech companies in a big "flour shortage" crisis where they struggle to build their computers.
